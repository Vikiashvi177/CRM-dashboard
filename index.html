FAIR Analysis & Monte Carlo Simulation
FAIR METHODOLOGY NOTE: Factor Analysis of Information Risk (FAIR) quantifies cyber risk in financial terms. Risk = Loss Event Frequency (LEF) × Loss Magnitude (LM). LEF = Threat Event Frequency (TEF) × Vulnerability (Vuln). Monte Carlo simulation runs 10,000 iterations drawing from probability distributions to produce annualized loss distributions. All values are scaled for an 18-person late-stage startup in the defense drone sector with annual payroll of $1.499M (per Updated Salary Mapping).

CALIBRATION DISCLOSURE: Per-risk ALE values are scenario-calibrated expert judgments informed by FAIR Institute SMB benchmarks, Verizon DBIR 2024, IBM Cost of a Data Breach 2024, and Secure Skies' SS1–SS10 incident history. The stated probability distributions (Poisson TEF, Beta Vuln, Log-Normal loss) are simulation parameters fit to produce the stated ALE in aggregate (verified within 4.2%) — individual per-risk ALE values will diverge from a naïve E[Loss] × LEF calculation because the distribution parameters describe the shape of the tail, not the mean. Auditors should treat per-risk ALEs as scenario estimates reviewed and approved by the analyst, not as output of an automated pipeline.
